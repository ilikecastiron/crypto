The most interesting thing to me about this project was finding out how many different levels and types of encryption there are. I had no clue that there would be such a wide range of difficulties and types of ciphers. I'm sure that this only covers the tip of the iceburg too.

  • encryption - The process of concealing information to only only selected parties can access it.
  • decryption -
  • cipher - The reverse of above, being able to reveal the encoding information.
  • plaintext - Information that has not been encrypted.
  • ciphertext - The final result of plaintext being encrypted.
  • key - The piece of information that determines the process taken to decrypt information.
  • private key - The ability for only the owner of the key to be able to decrypt information.
  • public key - The ability for anyone to be able to encrypt information.
  • digital signature - The process of authenticating information to ensure security during transit.
  • one time pad - The ultimate encryption process. Cannot be cracked and ensures no fingerprint is left behind. The key is never repeated and requires each character of the plaintext to be decrypted one by one.
